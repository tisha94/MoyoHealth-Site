Welcome to the MOYO Health Network!

"MOYO" Swahili for "heart", and we have a heart for the community. We are committed to everyone thriving with a healthy heart, and we mean EVERYONE!

Our MOYO team includes creators from the Morehouse School of Medicine, Emory University, Georgia Institute of Technology and the Community.
Our first project, the MOYO Health App, is being piloted now. Join us! Click on one the images below to find out more.

Experience

Opportunity to be a part of an innovative project that may lead to decreased heart disease rates. Some participants may be selected to learn coding for mobile app design.

Features

mHealth and IoT technologies will be used to identify any changes in heart health-related behaviors, including physical activity, sleep quality, food choices and mental/emotional health .

Innovations

We aim to reduce the risk of heart disease by empowering young adults to embrace behaviors that can improve heart health through creative, digital health strategies (Mobile Health (mHealth) technologies).

Health Equity

Since cell phones are used equally among Americans, the cell phone-based health technologies are within equal reach of everyone. Mobile health projects like this one are important to the future of health care.

About Moyo Health App




The Cardiovascular Research Institute of Morehouse School of Medicine has initiated an electronic cohort in collaboration with Emory University and Georgia Tech, and the program is currently piloting now. The program has another unique element, which brings job training, work ethics, and other critical skills to young people ages 18-29 who have a high school diploma but no undergraduate degree. This pipeline program is being conducted in partnership with Tech Square Labs. The first four graduates are currently interning in this program.
